# Writing Style Guide for Karen

## General Observations
- **Voice & Tone:** Casual, practical, and conversational. Reads like a knowledgeable friend explaining a tech project or sharing a personal discovery. It is grounded in personal experience and curiosity.
- **Paragraph Style:** Short to medium length (2-4 sentences max). Highly readable and avoids walls of text.
- **Heading Usage:** Uses H2 (`##`) for main sections and H3 (`###`) for subsections. Headings are descriptive, straightforward, and sometimes posed as questions (e.g., "Seperti apakah musik City Pop itu?").
- **Sentence Structure:** A mix of short, punchy statements and medium-length explanatory sentences. Active voice is strongly preferred.
- **Technical Explanations:** Step-by-step and logical. Often highlights specific obstacles and immediately provides the solution (e.g., using bolded "**[Kendala #1:]()**").
- **Use of Examples:** Heavily relies on markdown tables for specs/comparisons, code blocks for scripts/configs, and blockquotes for notes/warnings/updates.
- **Formatting Habits:** 
  - **Bold** is used for emphasis, UI elements, or key takeaways.
  - *Italics* are strictly used for English/foreign terms in the Indonesian text.
  - Images ALWAYS use the custom shortcode `{{< img src="..." width="800" alt="..." caption="..." >}}`.
- **Post Length:** Concise and to the point, typically ranging from 300 to 600 words.
- **Closing/Conclusion:** Brief wrap-up summarizing the takeaway or final thought. Frequently ends with the signature sign-off: "Stay curious! ✨" or a relevant single emoji.

## Indonesian Writing Style
- **Informal/Formal Mixing:** Uses standard, polite Indonesian (Bahasa baku) but maintains a conversational flow. Uses "saya" for self and "kamu/kalian" for the reader.
- **Vocabulary & Loanwords:** Seamlessly integrates English tech terms, which are always italicized (e.g., *security update*, *software*, *driver*). 
- **Slang & Affixes:** Uses casual affixes and conversational markers naturally without being overly slangy (e.g., "nge-trend", "nge-game", "di-support", "meng-inject", "lumayan", "pas-pasan").
- **Humor & Personality:** Self-aware and mildly self-deprecating. 
  - *Example:* "Saya pilih USB stick sebagai media instalasi, sebagaimana orang 'waras' lainnnya di tahun 2025."
  - *Example:* "...bila di lain waktu saya membeli Mini PC ini, maka ini bukan karena penghematan, tapi karena penasaran 😝"

## English Writing Style
- **Voice Translation:** The English version mirrors the Indonesian structure closely but adapts idioms to sound natural. It retains the personal, experiential tone.
- **ESL Patterns:** Generally fluent, but occasionally features slight Indonesian-influenced phrasing or literal translations that add to the personal voice rather than detracting from it.
  - *Example:* "How could it not be?" (Literal translation of "Bagaimana tidak").
  - *Example:* "The P106-100 unit I got was quite pitiful..." (Translation of "cukup mengenaskan").
- **Vocabulary:** Uses standard tech terminology comfortably. The tone is slightly more formal than the Indonesian version but still very accessible.

## Key Differences Between Languages
- **Italics Usage:** In Indonesian, English words are heavily italicized to denote foreign terms. In the English version, italics are used more sparingly, primarily for emphasis or specific titles/terms (e.g., *return on investment*, *hardware*).
- **Nuance:** The Indonesian version feels slightly more intimate and playful due to the natural inclusion of casual affixes (like "nge-game"). The English version is slightly more straightforward but keeps the same logical flow and personal anecdotes.

## Anti-Patterns — What I Do NOT Do
- **NO Corporate/Academic Speak:** Avoid words like "Furthermore", "Thus", "Moreover", "In conclusion", "Therefore".
- **NO AI Filler:** Never use generic introductions like "In today's fast-paced digital world..." or "Welcome back to my blog."
- **NO Walls of Text:** Do not write paragraphs longer than 4 sentences.
- **NO Over-explaining Basics:** Do not explain what a GPU is, what Hugo is, or basic markdown syntax unless it is the specific core topic of the post.
- **NO Standard Markdown Images:** Never use `![alt](url)`. Always use the `{{< img >}}` shortcode.
- **NO Made-up Facts:** Stick to the provided specs, hardware, and personal experiences.

## Sample Phrases and Patterns
- **Openings:**
  - "Sejak awal pandemi COVID-19 di tahun 2020 hingga sekarang, saya bekerja dari rumah." (Contextual/Personal)
  - "Windows 7 merupakan versi Windows yang bisa dibilang paling berhasil. Bagaimana tidak..." (Direct/Opinionated)
- **Transitions:**
  - "Untuk menjawab pertanyaan tersebut, mari coba kita hitung-hitung terlebih dahulu..."
  - "Untungnya, ada trick yang bisa digunakan untuk..."
  - "Lanjut..."
- **Closings:**
  - "Maka dari itu, bila di lain waktu saya membeli Mini PC ini, maka ini bukan karena penghematan, tapi karena penasaran 😝"
  - "Stay curious! ✨"
